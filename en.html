How it works

Three steps. Under 90 seconds.

Sign in once. Connect WhatsApp once. Then forget about it — and just text expenses the way you already text everyone else.

1

Sign in with Google

We create a private sheet in your Drive. We use the most restricted Google scope (drive.file) — we can't see anything else.

2

Connect WhatsApp

Tap once to open a chat with our bot. Say "hi" — you're in. The bot remembers you across devices.

3

Text your expenses

"50 grocery" · "12 coffee" · "today?" — works in English, Hebrew, or mixed.

Why people stay

Most trackers die in two weeks.

Ours is built around the one thing you already do every day. Open WhatsApp. Type a number. Done.

No app to install

WhatsApp is already on your phone. The fewer new things you have to learn, the longer the habit lasts.

Your sheet, your data

It's a Google Sheet in your own Drive. Export anytime. Cancel anytime — your data stays exactly where it is.

Smart categorization

"Wolt" maps to Restaurants. "Super-Pharm" to Health. "AWS" to Subscriptions. Learns the shortcuts you use.

Hebrew, English, mixed

Type in whatever language is fastest. "50 cafe" and "50 קפה" land in the same row.

Family sharing

Up to four phones write to one shared sheet, with per-person color tags. No more "who paid for what."

Weekly digest

Every Sunday morning, a summary lands in your WhatsApp. Spikes flagged. Trends visible. Five seconds to read.

New

Splitwise-style group expenses

Roommates, family trips, dinners. Type "כספלה צור" to start a group, share a 6-character code with your friends, then anyone in the group can record a shared expense with "כספלה 245 super". The bot tracks who paid what, who owes whom, and suggests the minimum-transfer plan to settle up. Learn more →
